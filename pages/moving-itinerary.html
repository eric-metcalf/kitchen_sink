↓ Before You Leave · SF Packing Window

Three days to pack, purge,
and hand over the keys.

Build in at least three full days before May 28 for the SF side. The actual packing goes faster than expected — the selling, donating, and decision-making takes the real time. May 25–27 gives a Monday departure with a clear weekend to wrap everything up without a last-night panic.

Day 1 · May 25 (Sat)
Purge & sell
  • List furniture on Facebook Marketplace (price to move fast)
  • Bag donations for Goodwill or SFSPCA thrift
  • Decide what's worth shipping vs. replacing in Boston
  • Buy packing supplies: boxes, tape, bubble wrap, markers
  • Take photos of electronics setups before disassembling
Day 2 · May 26 (Sun)
Pack room by room
  • Books and media first (heaviest — bottom of boxes)
  • Label every box: room + contents + fragile if needed
  • Pack a "first night" bag that stays accessible in the car
  • Wrap dishes and fragile items individually
  • Confirm container / truck pickup window with provider
Day 3 · May 27 (Mon)
Load & close out
  • Load container or truck — heavy items first, against the wall
  • Walk every room, closet, and cabinet one final time
  • Clean apartment; photograph condition for deposit return
  • Return keys; confirm forwarding address with post office
  • Fill car with road trip gear; charge everything overnight
1.
Wednesday, May 28
San Francisco→Salt Lake City
750 miles
~10.5 hrs driving
Depart early (7 am)
7:00 am
Departure
San FranciscoCA

Leave early to beat the Bay Bridge backup. Take I-80 east — you'll cross the Bay Bridge, then the Carquinez, then you're out of the Bay Area and climbing into the Sierra Nevada foothills within 90 minutes. This is the last morning in California. Take it in.

10:30 am
Fuel + Stretch
Truckee / Donner PassCA

The summit of Donner Pass sits at 7,135 feet. Stop at the Donner Memorial State Park scenic overlook if weather permits — the Sierras are at their best in late May with snowcaps still visible. Fuel in Truckee before the Nevada desert stretch.

12:30 pm
Lunch Stop
RenoNV

Reno is a solid lunch stop — get off at Virginia Street for a quick meal. The city has better food than its reputation suggests. The Old Granite Street Eatery or grab supplies at the Whole Foods if you'd rather push on. Fill up on gas here; Nevada fuel stops thin out heading east.

4:30 pm
Afternoon Drive-Through
ElkoNV

The I-80 corridor through Nevada is long, flat, and genuinely beautiful in a stark way — sagebrush valleys flanked by distant mountain ranges. Elko breaks the monotony at roughly the state's midpoint. Commercial Casino Basque restaurant if you're hungry; Picon Punch is the local tradition.

7:30 pm
Overnight
Salt Lake CityUT

You'll arrive tired. SLC has excellent food and a surprisingly good hotel scene — The Grand America is the classic, but the Marriott downtown is solid and cheaper. The city sits in a stunning bowl between the Wasatch and Oquirrh ranges; the morning view from a high floor is worth it.

Road Notes
◈ I-80 all the way. The most direct coast-to-coast interstate. Straightforward but long through Nevada.
◈ Donner Pass: no chains required in late May, but can see snow flurries — check Caltrans before leaving.
◈ Nevada fuel: long gaps between exits. Don't let the tank drop below half.
2.
Thursday, May 29
Salt Lake City→Denver
525 miles
~7.5 hrs driving
Lighter day
8:00 am
Departure
Salt Lake CityUT

A more relaxed morning — you earned it. Breakfast at the hotel or grab something on the way out. Take I-15 south briefly, then catch I-80 west to the Bonneville Speedway exit, or head straight onto I-80 east toward the Wyoming border depending on your mood.

9:00 am
Optional Detour — Worth It
Bonneville Salt FlatsUT

Forty minutes west of SLC on I-80, the Bonneville Salt Flats are genuinely otherworldly — a 30,000-acre expanse of white salt that in spring can hold a thin sheet of water, creating a mirror effect that's one of the stranger landscapes in North America. Walk out on the flats. Takes 30–45 minutes total. Backtrack on I-80 to I-15 south to US-6 east into Colorado.

12:00 pm
Scenic Drive + Lunch
Provo / Spanish Fork CanyonUT

US-6 east from Spanish Fork through Price Canyon is one of the most beautiful drives in Utah — red-rock walls, small mining towns, the Book Cliffs hovering on the horizon. Lunch in Price or Green River. Ray's Tavern in Green River is a classic road stop; famous for green-chile burgers.

2:30 pm
State Line + Scenic Stretch
Grand JunctionCO

Colorado begins here and it announces itself immediately — the Colorado National Monument rises to the south. From Grand Junction, I-70 east climbs into the Rockies via Glenwood Canyon, one of the most dramatic highway stretches in the US: 12 miles of red-rock gorge with the Colorado River beside you. Slow down and drive it properly.

6:30 pm
Overnight
DenverCO

Denver is a legitimate city worth a solid dinner. Rioja in Larimer Square or Tavernetta near Union Station are both excellent. The 16th Street Mall is pedestrian-friendly if you want to walk off the drive. Stay near downtown — Capitol Hill or Union Station area puts you in easy highway position for the morning.

Road Notes
◈ Glenwood Canyon: I-70 closes occasionally for rockfall or flooding. Check CDOT before leaving Grand Junction.
◈ Vail Pass at 10,662 ft and Eisenhower Tunnel at 11,013 ft — the highest interstate tunnels in the US. Snow possible even in late May.
◈ Denver altitude is 5,280 ft — the "Mile High" thing is real. Drink extra water.
3.
Friday, May 30
Denver→Kansas City
605 miles
~8.5 hrs driving
Longest flat drive
7:30 am
Departure
DenverCO

Early start — today is long and Kansas is wide. Take I-70 east out of Denver. The Rockies shrink in the rearview over about 45 minutes as the Front Range drops into the High Plains. It's a dramatic transition: you will watch the last mountains disappear and the Great Plains swallow you.

8:30 am
Quick Stop
LimonCO

The last real services before Kansas. Fuel here. Limon sits at the exact point where Colorado's landscape fully flattens into prairie — it looks like the edge of the world. Fill the tank, grab coffee, and settle in: 400 miles of Kansas await.

12:30 pm
Lunch + Midpoint
SalinaKS

The geographic and psychological midpoint of the day. Salina is a proper small city with good lunch options — the Brookville Hotel has been serving fried chicken since 1915 and is a Kansas institution. The Flint Hills begin here: rolling tallgrass prairie that was burned each spring for millennia by Plains tribes and lightning. In late May, the hills glow bright green.

3:30 pm
Quick Stretch
TopekaKS

State capital, unremarkable from the highway, useful for fuel. The Brown v. Board of Education National Historic Site is here if you want a short detour (20 min roundtrip from I-70) — it's in the actual Monroe Elementary School building. Worth a stop if there's any interest in American history.

5:30 pm
Overnight
Kansas CityMO

Kansas City is an underrated road trip stop. Dinner must be BBQ: Arthur Bryant's (the original, since 1958) or Gates Bar-B-Q if you want the slightly spicier version. Stay in the Crossroads Arts District or downtown — both are walkable and well-priced. KC is also straddling two time zones, so confirm you're on Central before setting alarms.

Road Notes
◈ I-70 Kansas is genuinely tedious. Good podcast day. No significant mountains or curves for 400 miles.
◈ Late May brings tornado season to the southern Plains. Watch weather apps, especially in the afternoon.
◈ Time zone change: Kansas is Central (same as KC). Set clocks forward 1 hr when crossing into KC.
4.
Saturday, May 31
Kansas City→Indianapolis
480 miles
~6.5 hrs driving
St. Louis detour
8:30 am
Departure
Kansas CityMO

I-70 east again. Missouri is a pleasant surprise after Kansas — rolling hills, forests, the Missouri River running alongside parts of the highway. The 250 miles to St. Louis go quickly.

11:30 am
Worthy Stop — 1.5 hrs
St. LouisMO

The Gateway Arch is one of the best pieces of American public infrastructure — 630 feet of stainless steel on the bank of the Mississippi, marking the point where America turned west. Take the tram to the top: there's nothing else like the view of the Mississippi and the Illinois flatlands from inside the Arch's narrow peak. Budget 90 minutes including parking. Toasted Ravioli is the local food you should eat once.

2:00 pm
Illinois Drive-Through
Terre HauteIN

Cross the Mississippi (one of the great river crossings in America — look both ways), enter Illinois, drive I-70 east through farm country. Terre Haute is the fuel and coffee stop before Indianapolis. Indiana begins here and it looks exactly like Illinois: flat, agricultural, good.

4:30 pm
Overnight
IndianapolisIN

Indianapolis punches above its weight as a road trip stop. The Mass Ave and Fountain Square neighborhoods have genuinely good restaurants and bars — Tinker Street and Milktooth are both worth finding. Stay downtown; the Bottleworks Hotel (in a converted Coca-Cola bottling plant) is the most interesting option in the city.

5.
Sunday, June 1
Indianapolis→Pittsburgh
375 miles
~5.5 hrs driving
Easy day, rest up
9:00 am
Departure
IndianapolisIN

Slow morning — this is the shortest drive day. Eat a proper breakfast. Take I-70 east out of Indy into Ohio; the landscape gradually gets hillier as you approach the Appalachian foothills. Ohio goes fast.

11:30 am
Lunch + Stretch
ColumbusOH

Columbus is a genuinely underrated American city with a strong food scene. The Short North neighborhood is good for a late brunch — Katalina's has a line but is worth it for the pancake balls. Or stay on I-70 and stop at the Polaris area exit for something fast. You only need 45–60 minutes here.

2:00 pm
Quick Stop
Cambridge / ZanesvilleOH

Eastern Ohio is underestimated — the landscape starts to roll and fold as the Appalachian Plateau begins. Zanesville sits on the junction of the Muskingum and Licking Rivers and has a famous Y-bridge (one of only a few in the world). A 15-minute detour if you like bridges.

3:30 pm
Overnight
PittsburghPA

Pittsburgh is one of the great American cities and the most underappreciated stop on this route. The approach from I-376 through the Fort Pitt Tunnel is famous: you emerge from the mountain into the downtown skyline hanging above three rivers. Primanti Bros. is obligatory (sandwiches with fries inside them). Stay in the Strip District or Downtown. Early arrival means a real evening in a real city before the final push tomorrow.

6.
Monday, June 2
Pittsburgh→Boston
580 miles
~8.5 hrs driving
Final day — longest finish
7:30 am
Departure
PittsburghPA

Early start — today is the longest final-day drive of the trip. Take I-376 east to the Pennsylvania Turnpike (I-76), then I-476 north toward Allentown rather than pushing through Philadelphia. This keeps you out of Philly traffic and puts you on I-78 east into New Jersey, then I-287 north to I-84 east — the cleanest path to New England.

10:00 am
Fuel + Stretch
Allentown / BethlehemPA

Two and a half hours out of Pittsburgh, Allentown and Bethlehem sit side by side in the Lehigh Valley. Historic Bethlehem Steel — the old mill site is now a museum and arts complex visible from the highway — is worth a slow drive-by even if you don't stop. Fuel here before catching I-78 east into New Jersey.

12:30 pm
Lunch
HartfordCT

Cross the Hudson via I-84, enter Connecticut, and stop in Hartford for lunch. The city is unremarkable but convenient — the highway services around exit 44 are fast and predictable. If time allows, Treva in West Hartford (10 minutes off I-84) is a far better meal. Hartford is almost exactly the halfway point between Pittsburgh and Boston: 290 miles each way.

2:30 pm
Scenic Stretch
Berkshire MountainsMA

The Massachusetts border and the Berkshires arrive almost simultaneously — rolling forested hills, old stone walls, small towns with white clapboard churches. I-90 (the Mass Pike) enters here and runs straight east to Boston. In late May / early June, the Berkshires are intensely green. This is New England properly. Roll the windows down.

4:30 pm
Arrival
BostonMA

The Mass Pike drops you into the city through the Ted Williams Tunnel or the Copley/Prudential exits depending on which neighborhood you're headed to. If driving a moving truck, note that Storrow Drive is strictly prohibited for trucks (low bridges — this is not a suggestion, it's a very real hazard). Take I-93 or Surface Artery routes instead. Park, breathe, eat something good. 3,315 miles. Six days. Done.

Road Notes
◈ Storrow Drive: absolutely no trucks. The 10-foot overpasses destroy U-Hauls every year. Use I-93 or Surface Artery to reach most Boston neighborhoods.
◈ Mass Pike tolls: ~$8–14 from the NY border to Boston. E-ZPass works across all Northeast states.
◈ Boston street parking for a large vehicle: confirm unloading logistics with your landlord before arrival — many blocks require permits or have narrow access.
↓ After You Arrive · Boston Unpack Window

Three days to unpack,
settle in, and make it home.

Don't try to unpack everything on arrival day — you'll be exhausted after 580 miles and the city will be disorienting. Give it three structured days: essentials first, then systems, then the administrative tasks that are easy to defer but genuinely important. By June 5, the apartment should feel lived-in rather than camped-in.

Day 1 · June 2 (Mon)
Arrive & unload
  • Unload the car and truck or accept container delivery
  • Get the bed set up before anything else — non-negotiable
  • Towels, toiletries, and one set of dishes accessible
  • Locate the nearest grocery store; stock the fridge minimally
  • Order dinner. You've earned it.
Day 2 · June 3 (Tue)
Build the kitchen
  • Unpack kitchen fully — this makes the apartment feel functional
  • Assemble any furniture that needs it (bed frame, shelves)
  • Hang shower curtain; find all the light switches
  • Return rental truck if applicable
  • Walk the neighborhood — find the coffee shop, the park, the market
Day 3 · June 4 (Wed)
Admin & settle
  • Update address: USPS, bank, DMV, subscriptions
  • Transfer utilities and internet to new address
  • Find a new primary care doctor and pharmacy
  • Get a Charlie Card (Boston's transit card) at any T station
  • Unpack the remaining boxes at a comfortable pace
